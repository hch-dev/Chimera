What is Phishing?

5 minute read

Understanding the Digital Threat

Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers disguise themselves as trustworthy entities to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, credit card numbers, and personal identification details.

How Phishing Works

  • Attackers create fake emails, websites, or messages
  • They impersonate legitimate organizations (banks, social media, etc.)
  • Victims are directed to click malicious links or download attachments
  • Personal information is stolen and used for fraud

Common Targets

  • Banking and financial accounts
  • Email and social media credentials
  • Corporate login information
  • Personal identification data

Phishing Attack Flow

Attacker creates fake message
→
Victim receives convincing email
→
Victim clicks malicious link
→
Information is stolen

Key Takeaways

Always verify sender identity before responding to requests
Check URLs carefully for misspellings or suspicious domains
Legitimate organizations rarely create urgent situations

Types of Phishing Attacks

8 minute read

Spear Phishing

Highly targeted attacks against specific individuals or organizations using personalized information.

Examples:

  • Emails referencing recent purchases
  • Messages using your name and job title
  • Requests referencing specific company projects

Clone Phishing

Attackers create identical copies of legitimate websites and emails to harvest credentials.

Examples:

  • Fake banking login pages
  • Duplicated social media sites
  • Counterfeit email login portals

SMishing (SMS Phishing)

Phishing attacks conducted through text messages, often containing malicious links.

Examples:

  • Package delivery scams
  • Bank account alerts
  • Prize winning notifications

Vishing (Voice Phishing)

Phone-based phishing attacks where attackers impersonate legitimate organizations.

Examples:

  • Fake tech support calls
  • Bank fraud department impersonation
  • Government agency scams

How to Identify Fake Websites

6 minute read

URL Inspection

What to Check:

  • Domain spelling: Look for subtle misspellings (gogle.com vs google.com)
  • HTTPS: Ensure the site uses secure encryption
  • Subdomains: Be wary of unusual subdomain structures
  • URL shorteners: Avoid clicking shortened links from unknown sources
Real vs Fake Examples:
✓ Legitimate: https://www.chase.com
✗ Fake: https://www.chase-login.com

SSL Certificate Check

How to Verify:

  • Look for the padlock icon in the address bar
  • Click the padlock to view certificate details
  • Verify if the certificate matches the domain
  • Check for certificate validity and issuer
Even sites with valid SSL certificates can be malicious - always verify the domain!

Visual Inspection

Red Flags:

  • Poor design quality: Low-resolution images, broken layouts
  • Grammar errors: Typos and poor language quality
  • Missing elements: Incomplete navigation, broken links
  • Outdated content: Old dates, expired promotions

How to Secure Personal Data

7 minute read

Online Behavior

Limit Information Sharing

Only provide necessary information on websites and social media. Avoid oversharing personal details that could be used for social engineering.

Use Privacy Settings

Regularly review and update privacy settings on social media and online accounts to control who can see your information.

Avoid Public WiFi

Refrain from accessing sensitive accounts on public WiFi networks. Use VPN services when connecting to unfamiliar networks.

Technical Protection

Install Security Software

Use reputable antivirus and anti-malware software. Keep all security software updated with the latest definitions.

Regular Updates

Keep your operating system, browsers, and applications updated to protect against known vulnerabilities.

Data Backup

Regularly backup important data to secure locations. Use cloud storage with encryption for sensitive files.

Password Protection Best Practices

5 minute read

Creating Strong Passwords

Length: At least 12-16 characters
Complexity: Mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols
Uniqueness: Different passwords for each account
Avoid: Personal information, common words, keyboard patterns

Password Management Tools

Password Managers

Applications that securely store and generate strong passwords. Examples include LastPass, 1Password, and Bitwarden.

Two-Factor Authentication

Add an extra layer of security with codes from apps like Google Authenticator or Authy.

Biometric Options

Use fingerprint or facial recognition when available for convenient and secure access.

Safe Browsing Tips

4 minute read
1

Verify Before You Click

Hover over links to preview the actual URL before clicking. Be skeptical of unsolicited emails and messages.

2

Use Bookmarks

Bookmark frequently visited legitimate sites instead of searching for them each time to avoid landing on fake versions.

3

Enable Browser Security

Activate built-in browser security features like phishing protection and malicious site warnings.

4

Regular Security Checkups

Periodically review your account activity and security settings. Look for suspicious login attempts.

5

Educate Yourself Continuously

Stay informed about new phishing tactics and security threats. Share knowledge with family and friends.

6

Trust Your Instincts

If something seems suspicious or too good to be true, it probably is. Take time to verify before acting.
